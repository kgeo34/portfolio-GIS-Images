I was lucky to live in Istanbul for many years. I was even luckier to live in Üsküdar for part of that time. Istanbul sits along two sides of the Bosphorus Strait, which connects the Black Sea to the north with the Marmara Sea to the south. The strait generally flows north to south but zig-zags its way through, which makes it disorienting, until sunset. At sunset, suddenly you know which direction you and everyone else are facing. Üsküdar is on the east side (Asian side) of the strait, and allows views of the Golden Horn (Sultanahmet) and Karaköy/Beyoğlu, both of which are on the European side. Near the shore the Maiden's Tower sits on a tiny island. This is the most popular spot to watch the sunset in Istanbul. As the sky darkens, the Maiden's Tower brightens. Something I always like about the water here is the turbulent and unpredictable currents and small, choppy surface waves mixing. It can seem like a powerful river and also a bubbling pot of soup at the same time. If you have ever been near a massive waterfall as the water approaches the waterfall, it has that same feeling. Watching that effect during sunset is really cool. This was my second painting. I think the details can be improved upon greatly, but I am very happy with the effect, atmosphere, and color gradient. Anyone who has been here for sunset would understand. Acrylic on canvas
